# Speaker Notes: Punjab GeoMethane + Machinery Analytics Presentation

*To be presented alongside the interactive GEE Dashboard or the 7 exported slide images.*

---

## Slide 1: Punjab Methane Map
**(Dashboard Layer: Methane Emissions toggled ON)**

**Key Message:** Village-level methane mapping is now available across Punjab.
- "We have built Punjab's first village-level methane and machinery decision-support system."
- "What you are looking at is a wall-to-wall footprint of agricultural emissions across all 12,467 villages."
- "This map eliminates the guesswork of where methane hotspots are concentrated, providing a baseline for where interventions are most urgently needed."

---

## Slide 2: Machinery Coverage
**(Dashboard Layer: In-Situ / Ex-Situ toggled ON)**

**Key Message:** Machinery deployment data could be verified for 3,339 villages.
- "These green villages highlight where we have verified machinery registry records successfully linked to village boundaries."
- "The villages shown in gray represent 'Data Not Available'. We explicitly isolated these 9,128 villages to ensure we do not mistakenly claim zero deployment where registry data simply failed to link due to spelling or naming mismatches."
- "This ensures every conclusion we present today is grounded in verified, honest data."

---

## Slide 3: Government Scheme Penetration
**(Dashboard Layer: Scheme Score toggled ON)**

**Key Message:** Visualizing CRM, SMAM, and CDP investments.
- "This layer aggregates public investment. The darker the red, the more government subsidies (CRM + SMAM + CDP) have penetrated that specific village."
- "By toggling this layer against the methane map, we can instantly see if our investments are physically reaching the highest-emitting areas."

---

## Slide 4: Policy Zones
**(Dashboard Layer: Village Policy Zones toggled ON)**

**Key Message:** High machinery deployment areas are also high methane areas.
- "Our analysis indicates that machinery deployment is heavily concentrated in higher-methane regions. However, we do not observe evidence that deployment alone is associated with lower methane levels in this cross-sectional snapshot."
- "Therefore, this red layer identifies 'Policy Failure Zones'—areas where heavy machinery subsidies have failed to lower methane emissions below the median."
- "Conversely, the blue 'Intervention Success Zones' highlight where high machinery presence aligns with unusually low emissions. We need to study what these villages are doing differently."

---

## Slide 5: Priority Villages
**(Dashboard Layer: Top 20 Priority Villages toggled ON)**

**Key Message:** Tactical targeting for future budgets.
- "If the department can only fund a limited pilot or intervention program next year, these specific locations represent the greatest potential value."
- "They were identified forensically based on the largest gaps between high methane emissions and low verified machinery."

---

## Slide 6: Intervention Priority Index
**(Dashboard Layer: Intervention Priority Index toggled ON)**

**Key Message:** A data-driven blueprint for budget allocation.
- "This is the core Decision Support Layer."
- "It automatically scores villages from Low Priority (Green) to Very High Priority (Red) by combining Methane levels, Machinery scarcity, and historical Policy Failure."
- "Rather than proving past intervention effectiveness, this dashboard serves as an optimization engine to prioritize exactly where the next wave of government funding should go."

---

## Slide 7: Known Limitations
**(Visual: Summary bullet points)**

**Key Message:** Scientific transparency and defensibility.
- "Before concluding, we must state the boundaries of this data:"
  - "Machinery registry linkage is available for 3,339 villages. The remainder are treated as 'Data Not Available', not zeros."
  - "This analysis is a cross-sectional snapshot from 2020."
  - "These are observed associations and do not imply causation."
  - "The dashboard supports prioritization, not impact evaluation."
  - "Multi-year monitoring is recommended to truly measure whether new machines reduce methane over time."
