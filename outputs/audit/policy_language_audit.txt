POLICY LANGUAGE AUDIT
=====================
Date: June 16, 2026

PURPOSE:
To ensure all project recommendations, reports, and presentations maintain strict scientific integrity and do not overstate causal relationships.

ISSUES IDENTIFIED AND CORRECTED:
1. OVERSTATEMENT: "Machinery reduces methane."
   CORRECTION: "Machinery deployment is associated with observed methane patterns."
   REASONING: Cross-sectional observational data cannot prove causation, only spatial correlation.

2. OVERSTATEMENT: "CRM failed in these villages."
   CORRECTION: "These villages represent Policy Failure Zones, where high CRM distribution coincides with high methane emissions, requiring further investigation."
   REASONING: We do not know if the machines were misused, sold, or if local geography naturally emits more CH4 regardless of CRM.

3. OVERSTATEMENT: "Investing in Priority Villages will lower methane."
   CORRECTION: "Targeting Priority Villages represents the highest potential value for intervention based on current methane-machinery gaps."
   REASONING: Avoids guaranteeing outcomes, framing it as an optimization strategy instead.

VERDICT:
All outgoing language must be scrubbed to reflect "associations" and "priority zones" rather than definitive "causation" and "guaranteed reductions."
