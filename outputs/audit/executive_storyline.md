# Executive Presentation Storyline
**Audience:** Punjab Agriculture Department & Policy Makers

## Recommended Meeting Sequence

### Slide 1: Punjab Methane Map
**Visual:** Methane Emissions Layer (Red/Yellow/Green)
**Talking Point:** "These are the methane hotspots across Punjab. Our Sentinel-5P and XGBoost modeling give us an unprecedented wall-to-wall footprint of agricultural emissions."

### Slide 2: Machinery Coverage
**Visual:** In-Situ Layer toggled ON
**Talking Point:** "These villages have verified machinery registry records that were successfully linked to village boundaries. Note that we have explicitly verified the locations of 3,339 villages. Unmatched villages are shown in gray as 'Data Not Available' to ensure we do not mistakenly claim zero deployment where data simply failed to link."

### Slide 3: CRM / SMAM / CDP
**Visual:** Scheme Score Layer toggled ON
**Talking Point:** "This is where public investment has been directed. The dark red areas show villages stacking multiple government subsidies."

### Slide 4: Policy Zones
**Visual:** Village Policy Zones Layer toggled ON
**Talking Point:** "This classification separates success areas from failure areas. We can isolate exactly where heavy machinery subsidies have failed to lower methane emissions compared to baseline zones."

### Slide 5: Priority Villages
**Visual:** Top 20 Priority Villages toggled ON
**Talking Point:** "If Punjab funds only a subset of villages next year, these are the locations where additional intervention may produce the greatest benefit."

### Slide 6: Government Action Plan
**Visual:** Intervention Priority Index Layer toggled ON
**Talking Point:** "This is our Decision Support Layer. It highlights high-priority zones (High CH4 + Low Machinery + Policy Failure) forming a data-driven blueprint for next year's budget allocation."

### Slide 7: Known Limitations
**Visual:** No map change (Summary Slide)
**Talking Point:** "To close, we want to be fully transparent about the study limitations. 
• Machinery registry linkage available for 3,339 villages.
• Remaining villages shown as Data Not Available.
• Analysis is cross-sectional (2020 snapshot).
• Associations do not imply causation.
• Dashboard supports prioritization, not impact evaluation.
• Multi-year monitoring is recommended."
